# 60-Second Oral Prompts

Use one timer minute per prompt. Speak without notes.

## Prompts

1. Explain open-loop vs closed-loop with one real-world example.
2. Explain what a transfer function is and why zero initial conditions matter.
3. Explain poles vs zeros in plain English.
4. Explain why phase margin relates to oscillation risk.
5. Explain Routh vs Nyquist in one contrast statement.
6. Explain continuous-time vs discrete-time stability conditions.
7. Explain controllability vs observability with physical intuition.
8. Explain why observer poles are typically faster.
9. Explain LQR vs MPC in constraint-heavy systems.
10. Explain one limitation of linearization.

## Scoring rubric (self)

- 2 points: accurate + clear + concise
- 1 point: partly accurate or vague
- 0 points: incorrect

Target: 16+/20 across 10 prompts.
