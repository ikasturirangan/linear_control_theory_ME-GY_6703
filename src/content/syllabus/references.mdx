# References and Citations

All syllabus modules cite these references using IDs like `[R1]`.

<h2 id="r1">[R1] Ogata, Modern Control Engineering</h2>
Katsuhiko Ogata, *Modern Control Engineering*, 5th Edition, Prentice Hall, 2010.
A primary textbook source for modeling, root locus, frequency response, and state-space topics.

<h2 id="r2">[R2] Franklin, Powell, Emami-Naeini</h2>
Gene F. Franklin, J. David Powell, Abbas Emami-Naeini, *Feedback Control of Dynamic Systems*, 8th Edition, Pearson, 2019.
Strong for performance specifications, design tradeoffs, and practical control interpretation.

<h2 id="r3">[R3] Åström and Murray (Open Text)</h2>
Karl J. Åström and Richard M. Murray, *Feedback Systems: An Introduction for Scientists and Engineers*, Princeton University Press, 2008.
Open online text: https://fbswiki.org/wiki/index.php/Feedback_Systems:_An_Introduction_for_Scientists_and_Engineers

<h2 id="r4">[R4] Dorf and Bishop</h2>
Richard C. Dorf and Robert H. Bishop, *Modern Control Systems*, 13th Edition, Pearson, 2016.
Broad source for classical and modern topics.

<h2 id="r5">[R5] MathWorks Simulink Documentation</h2>
Official Simulink documentation and examples:
https://www.mathworks.com/help/simulink/
Primary source for modeling workflows and simulation setup.

<h2 id="r6">[R6] Franklin, Powell, Workman (Digital Control)</h2>
Gene F. Franklin, J. David Powell, Michael L. Workman, *Digital Control of Dynamic Systems*, 3rd Edition, Addison-Wesley, 1998.
Used for sampled-data modeling, ZOH, and digital implementation concepts.

<h2 id="r7">[R7] Kuo and Golnaraghi</h2>
Benjamin C. Kuo and Farid Golnaraghi, *Automatic Control Systems*, 9th Edition, Wiley, 2010.
Useful for Routh/Jury methods and classical stability methods.

<h2 id="r8">[R8] Nise</h2>
Norman S. Nise, *Control Systems Engineering*, 8th Edition, Wiley, 2019.
Popular pedagogy for beginner-level explanation and step-by-step practice.

<h2 id="r9">[R9] MIT OpenCourseWare (Feedback and Systems)</h2>
MIT OpenCourseWare control/systems offerings and lecture notes:
https://ocw.mit.edu/

<h2 id="r10">[R10] Bryson and Ho</h2>
Arthur E. Bryson Jr. and Yu-Chi Ho, *Applied Optimal Control*, Taylor & Francis, 1975.
Classic source for LQR formulation.

<h2 id="r11">[R11] Camacho and Bordons</h2>
Eduardo F. Camacho and Carlos Bordons, *Model Predictive Control*, 2nd Edition, Springer, 2007.
Primary MPC reference for constrained optimal control.

## Citation style used in syllabus pages

Each module ends with a small citations block like:

- `[R1](/syllabus/references#r1)` for main theory
- `[R5](/syllabus/references#r5)` for software implementation notes
