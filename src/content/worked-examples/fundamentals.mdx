# Fundamentals Worked Example

## Problem

For a mass-spring-damper system:

$$
m\ddot{x}+c\dot{x}+kx=f(t)
$$

with $m=1$, $c=4$, $k=5$:

1. Derive transfer function $G(s)=X(s)/F(s)$.
2. Find poles.
3. State whether the plant is stable.

## Solution

### Step 1: Laplace transform

Assume zero initial conditions:

$$
(ms^2+cs+k)X(s)=F(s)
$$

Hence:

$$
G(s)=\frac{X(s)}{F(s)}=\frac{1}{ms^2+cs+k}=\frac{1}{s^2+4s+5}
$$

### Step 2: Pole calculation

Solve denominator:

$$
s^2+4s+5=0
$$

$$
s=\frac{-4\pm\sqrt{16-20}}{2}=-2\pm j
$$

### Step 3: Stability

Both poles have real part $-2<0$.
Therefore the open-loop plant is asymptotically stable.

## Extra interpretation

- Natural frequency: $\omega_n=\sqrt{5}$
- Damping ratio: $\zeta=\frac{4}{2\sqrt{5}}=\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\approx0.894$

This is a fairly well-damped second-order system.
